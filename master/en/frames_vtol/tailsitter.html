# Tailsitter VTOL

A Tailsitter VTOL takes off and lands on its tail, but flips into the fixed-wing orientation for normal flight. The tailsitter rotors are permanently fixed in position for forward flight.

Tailsitters are typically mechanically less complex than other types of VTOL, and hence may be less expensive to build and maintain. However they are aerodynamically more complex, and can be harder to tune and fly for hover and transition, particularly in windy conditions.

# Tailsitter Airframes

VTOL Duo Tailsitter
Two rotor VTOL that uses elevons to flip from hover into fixed-wing flight.

  • Forward flight more efficient
  • Hover harder to fly, particularly in wind
  • Hover and transition harder to tune
  • More compact form factor
VTOL Quad Tailsitter
Four rotor VTOL with optional elevons. Uses rotors to transition between modes (along with elevons if present).
  • Hover mode easier to fly and more stable
  • Less compact form factor (harder to transport)
  • "X" and "+" rotor configurations are supported (see Airframe Reference)

Duo Tailsitters are generally more efficient in cruise flight (4 smaller propellers are less efficient than 2 larger ones) and physically more compact. However as they are aerodynamically much more complex in hover mode, they are much harder to tune for both hover and transition. Quad tailsitters are easier to fly in hover mode, and more stable in windy conditions.

# Setup/Flying

VTOL setup and flying are covered in the VTOL parent topic.

Note

The instructions are essentially the same for all VTOL. The main frame-specific differences are the motor wiring (see VTOL Duo Tailsitter and VTOL Quad Tailsitter), and some aspects of tuning the configuration.
